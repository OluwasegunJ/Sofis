How SoFIS Works
The analytical process from data to recommendation
  • 1

    Household Energy Baseline

    Your monthly consumption (kWh) is estimated from your bill at the GRU residential rate of $0.14/kWh. Annual demand = monthly kWh x 12. This becomes the denominator for all offset calculations.

  • 2

    Energy Burden Calculation

    Energy burden = annual electricity cost divided by annual household income. HUD defines energy burdened as above 6% and severely burdened as above 10%. Low-income Gainesville households consume 35.6% less than the Florida statewide average. Burden is income-driven, not consumption-driven.

  • 3

    NREL PVWatts Solar Generation (Primary)

    The $10,000 capital constraint divided by NREL Q1 2024 benchmark of $2.74/W gives a 3.6 kW system (9 panels, 195 sq ft). PVWatts was run at 9 verified NE Gainesville addresses and returned a mean of 5,291 kWh/yr (62.0% offset). This is the primary, NREL-validated estimate.

  • 4

    Google Sunroof Conservative Reference

    Implied generation = usable sunlight hours x 3.6 kW x 0.80 performance ratio. Mean result: 3,815 kWh/yr (44.7%). The 28% gap between PVWatts and Sunroof reflects site-specific tree shading. Real performance falls between the two estimates.

  • 5

    Financing Scenario Analysis

    Monthly payment M = P[r(1+r)^n] / [(1+r)^n - 1] where P = $10,000. Net cash flow = monthly savings minus monthly payment. Five scenarios are tested from market-rate mortgages to community revolving loan funds, across 7-year and 30-year terms.

  • 6

    Lifecycle Cost Considerations

    Over a 30-year loan term, households should plan for: panel cleaning (3-4 times/year, community volunteer programs recommended), inverter replacement (typically once or twice at years 10-15), and roof replacement (solar adds panel removal and reinstall cost when insurance requires re-roofing).

About SoFIS
Solar Feasibility Intelligence System — University of Florida, 2026
SoFIS is a project deliverable of the Master of Science in Integrated Sustainable Development program at the University of Florida. It operationalizes the solar feasibility methodology for low-income owner-occupied housing in Gainesville, Florida for planners, community development practitioners, and homeowners.
