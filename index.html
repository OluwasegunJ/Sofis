How SoFIS Works
The analytical process from data to recommendation
  • 1
    Household Energy Baseline
    Your monthly kWh is estimated from your bill at $0.14/kWh. Annual demand = monthly kWh x 12. This is the denominator for all offset calculations.
  • 2
    Energy Burden Calculation
    Energy burden = annual electricity cost divided by annual household income. HUD defines energy burdened as above 6% and severely burdened as above 10%.
  • 3
    NREL PVWatts Solar Generation (Primary)
    $10,000 divided by $2.74/W (NREL Q1 2024) gives a 3.6 kW system. PVWatts modeled at 9 verified NE Gainesville addresses returned a mean of 5,291 kWh/yr (62.0% offset).
  • 4
    Google Sunroof Conservative Reference
    Implied generation = usable sunlight hours x 3.6 kW x 0.80 performance ratio. Mean: 3,815 kWh/yr (44.7%). The 28% gap reflects site-specific tree shading.
  • 5
    Financing Scenario Analysis
    Monthly payment M = P[r(1+r)^n] / [(1+r)^n - 1] where P = $10,000. Net cash flow = monthly savings minus monthly payment. Five scenarios tested across 7-year and 30-year terms.
  • 6
    Lifecycle Cost Considerations
    Budget for: panel cleaning (3-4x/year), inverter replacement (years 10-15), and roof-related panel removal/reinstall costs. Community volunteer programs can help keep maintenance costs low.

About SoFIS
University of Florida, 2026
SoFIS is a project deliverable of the Master of Science in Integrated Sustainable Development program at the University of Florida. It operationalizes the solar feasibility methodology for low-income owner-occupied housing in Gainesville, Florida.
